Why It Works

The Future Farmer isn’t a worksheet in disguise. It’s a strategic card game rooted in real forestry and sustainable agriculture science — built by a practicing forester. Students don’t just learn about sustainability, they make decisions about it.

🎓 Curriculum-Aligned

Maps to geography, environmental science, biology, and sustainability learning outcomes. Every card links to real ecological principles.

🎲 Built for Engagement

Strategic decision-making, risk assessment, and resource management keep students thinking critically — not just memorising facts.

✅ No Prep Required

Open the box, read the quick-start rules, and play. Teacher guides handle the rest — discussion prompts, learning objectives, and assessment rubrics included.

What Students Learn

The Future Farmer was designed by a practicing forester. Every card in the deck reflects real ecological dynamics — from how agroforestry systems build soil carbon to why monocultures collapse under pest pressure. Students don’t memorise definitions; they experience cause and effect through gameplay. When a Drought destroys an unprotected farm, or a well-placed Innovation saves one, the science sticks because it mattered in the moment. Here are six cards from the game and the real-world topics they open up.

Farm Module · +3 PES Food Forest

“An edible forest garden mimicking natural forest structure. Multiple layers of trees, shrubs, herbs, and ground cover. Open market 24/7.”

Students explore: Forest canopy layers, permaculture design, how vertical diversity maximises photosynthesis and food production on a single plot of land.

Farm Module · +2 PES Intercropping

“Grow two or more crops in the same field at the same time. Like a conga line at a wedding — better together.”

Students explore: Companion planting, nitrogen fixation, how biodiversity in agriculture reduces pest pressure and improves yields compared to monocultures.

Innovation · +1 PES No Tillage

“Do not disturb your downstairs neighbours. Plant directly into undisturbed soil to preserve structure and boost organic matter.”

Students explore: Soil biology, the role of fungi and earthworms, how conventional tilling releases stored carbon and degrades soil structure over time.

Innovation Water Harvesting

“All your Farm Modules are immune to Drought. Water for every farm.”

Students explore: The water cycle, rainwater capture techniques, how water scarcity drives conflict and migration, and why water management is central to climate adaptation.

Challenge Drought

“Destroy all Farm Modules of one opponent worth 3 or fewer credits. It’s a growing problem.”

Students explore: Climate change and extreme weather, why low-resilience farms fail first, and how diversification and water management create natural defences.

Farm Module · +5 PES Forest Conservation

“Preserve and protect forest ecosystems. Maintain biodiversity, prevent deforestation, and promote sustainable land use. Have you heard of wild pets?”

Students explore: Deforestation and its global impacts, ecosystem services, carbon sequestration, and why the highest-value card in the game is about protecting what already exists.
